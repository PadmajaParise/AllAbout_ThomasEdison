1847 - 1931

America's greatest inventor: Thomas Edison. He held over 1,000 patents for his inventions..

TIMELINE
  • 1847 - born in 1847 in Milan, Ohio, and grew up in Port Huron, Michigan.
  • 1866 - At the age of 19, Thomas Edison moved to Louisville, Kentucky, where, as an employee of Western Union, he worked the Associated Press bureau news wire.
  • 1867 - Thomas Edison is Fired from Western Union.
  • 1869 - Thomas Edison Patents the Electric Vote Recorder.
  • 1871 - Thomas Edison Marries Mary Stilwell.
  • 1876 - Thomas Edison Opens First Industrial Research Lab.
  • 1877 - Thomas Alva Edison Completes The Model For The First Phonograph.
  • 1878 - Thomas Edison Patents the First Phonograph.The phonograph was developed as a result of Thomas Edison's work on two other inventions, the telegraph and the telephone..
  • 1879 - Edison Improves Carbon Filament Lamp. Thomas Edison began serious research into developing a practical incandescent lamp.
  • 1880 - Edison Patents DC Electricity Distribution.Edison patented a system for electricity distribution in 1880, which was essential to capitalize on the invention of the electric lamp.
  • 1882 - Nikola Tesla begins working for Continental Edison. In 1882 Tesla moved to Paris, to work as an engineer for the Continental Edison Company, designing improvements to electric equipment brought overseas from Edison's ideas.
  • 1883 - In Britain, the Edison and Swan companies merged into the Edison and Swan United Electric Company (later known as Ediswan, which was ultimately incorporated into Thorn Lighting Ltd).
  • 1884 - Mary Edison Dies.On December 25, 1871, Edison had married 16-year-old Mary Stilwell, whom he had met two months earlier as she was an employee at one of his shops.
  • 1885 - Nikola Tesla quits working for Edison.Tesla claims he was offered US$50,000 (~ US$1.1 million in 2007, adjusted for inflation) if he redesigned Edison's inefficient motor and generators, making an improvement in both service and economy.
  • 1892 - General Electric is Established.By 1890, Thomas Edison had brought together several of his business interests under one corporation to form Edison General Electric.
  • 1931 - Edison was active in business right up to the end. Just months before his death in 1931, the Lackawanna Railroad implemented electric trains in suburban service from Hoboken to Gladstone, Montclair and Dover in New Jersey.
